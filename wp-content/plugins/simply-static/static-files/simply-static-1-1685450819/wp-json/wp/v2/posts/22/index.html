North Korea will launch a military satellite to monitor the military activities of the US and its allies. It will be launched for spying next June. State-owned media reported this Tuesday, quoting a senior defense official of the country.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Japan said on Monday that it has learned that Pyongyang may launch an artificial satellite this week.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

The country’s state-run Korean Central News Agency quoted Ri Pyong-chol, vice chairman of the Central Military Commission of North Korea’s ruling party, as saying that the ‘Military Reconnaissance Satellite Number One’ will be launched in June.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Earlier on April 13, North Korea tested a new intercontinental ballistic missile. According to the country’s government, it is a ‘solid fuel’ missile. The name is ‘Hwasong-18’.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

The missile is said to be the ‘most powerful’ in North Korea’s arsenal. According to the state-owned media KCNA, Hwasong-18 can be launched relatively quickly as a solid fuel missile. These missiles are difficult to detect and counter.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

North Korean leader Kim Jong-un said at the time that the successful test of the intercontinental ballistic missile Hwasong-18 would increase the fear and anxiety of rival countries.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Last March, a major joint military exercise ‘Freedom Shield 23’ was held between South Korea and the United States. It is the largest US-South Korea joint military exercise in five years. North Korea opposes this exercise. Before and after the exercise, the country showed its strength by firing one missile after another.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
